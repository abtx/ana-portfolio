Challenge

Financial responsibility is increasingly important among young people to achieve financial independence and stability, so I have designed this UI with young people’s finances in mind.

Account page view

Visual hierarchy

  • Adaptable view that uses an interactive timeline helps to simplify and present essential information in a way that is receptive of distinct needs and end-goals of users.
  • Visual representation of data using graphs and bars is designed to help users in a quick and effective identification of ratio between their income and outcome.

Function: The Overview function helps users in quick access to a summary of users income and outcome. Users can examine their spending across time.

User Oriented

  • Dynamic and responsive notifications allow users to move efficiently across the app to address problematic areas.
  • Directing users attention by implementing features that expand and highlight certain areas to help in guiding users through completing an action.

Function: Money Boxes function allows users to set personal or shared financial goals and collect money overtime by contributing directly to a money box or by scheduling a payment.

Payments page view
Accessible and simple architecture

Responsive

  • Collapsing and expanding menu enables users to reach key management features from any point in the app.
  • Responsive and accessible money and account management help users to control their accounts on demand.

Function: When using Drop Down Menu users can quickly overview their accounts, access and transfer funds between current and savings accounts and manage their accounts on demand.

Transparent

  • Simplified, visual elements such as colour coded bars or deadlines help users in a more effective understanding of their financial goals.
  • Scrollable list with key information about each card that expands into a detailed view on request.

Function: Money Boxes function allows users to set personal or shared financial goals and collect money overtime by contributing directly to a Money Box or by scheduling a payment.

Engaging

  • Fostering good financial habits by presenting users with visualisation of their goals by using photos and graphs.
  • Encouraging user participation by enabling users to interact and follow actions of other contributors to grow excitement around their shared financial goals.

Organised

Function: My Spending Habits feature helps users to understand and learn about the nature of their finances.

To enhance the user experience by designing clear and user-friendly interface and user interaction features. Embracing transparency and functionality of the app, my proposition promotes financial responsibility and independence.

Mimo.hue is a personal project of mine for which I create experimental and abstract artworks. The essence of Mimo.hue is to "Let the unexpected happen." I reflect that idea through the creative process and the aesthetics - flowing shapes, colourful gradients, and handmade textures.

The UI is intentionally minimalistic to drive focus on the artwork itself and let the user spend more time appreciating the artwork they might be inclined to buy. I have achieved that by dividing the flow into stages that make the interface expand and change gradually according to user actions.

Design toolkit for NHS hospitals

  • user research
  • layout design
  • prototyping and user testing
Organisations
The Glasgow School of Art, The University Hospital Crosshouse
Timeline
5 weeks in 2016
Software
Sketch, Photoshop, Principle

Challenge

To improve quality of work for hospital staff and experience of care for hospital patients through research and analysis of current patient flow. Finding pain points and corresponding design opportunities in the system, creating and testing solutions while working closely with a spectrum of stakeholders.

Insight cards layout
Research illustrations

Collaborating with other students, we were given a task to investigate patient flow in an NHS hospital. We were working collaboratively with NHS staff to find and analyse pain points that produced an overwhelming amount of insights.

After few field trips and presentations to the client, we became enlightened of how multi-layered this design challenge was. Dealing with this copious system and the dynamic nature of the involved processes, we approached the project from a perspective of advocating design thinking within the service.

Therefore, we designed a design toolkit based on our findings. The NHS staff can use the toolkit according to their own findings and insights gathered through working within the hospital system. To encourage empathy and improve communication among the staff members as well as their patients we proposed three tools. A role-play booklet, a ’communication triangle’ and a deck of insight cards to visualise problematic areas in a user-friendly way.

My key responsibility for this part of the project was to co-create layout for insight cards. The cards promote a user-centered approach by setting problems met by the staff and their patients in the centre of attention.

I have also demonstrated my drawing skills to draw friendly and engaging illustrations to communicate collected research. My illustration style received plenty of compliments for visualising complex and sensitive issues in an approachable way.

Software

Challenge

I like to see non-conforming people being empowered and their social mobility improved. Gender non-conformists are a group who struggles with this issue, in particular. I have started this project where I explore and respond to complex values, needs, and desires of this group within social, political and community contexts.

Ideation workshop with users

Through extensive research and engagement with community members, I have developed a forum app, a communication toolkit, and a pride badge. These tools connect and educate community members and their support network alike.

I have introduced these tools to inspire members of the gender non- conforming community to proudly embrace their unique identities and personal journey patterns of self-discovery.

Software

Challenge

For this project, I have explored an idea of long distance relationships that put a strain on the lives of family members, friends or lovers who live far away from each other. I have created an 'Alternative Now' scenario where Low Earth Orbit Satellite Technology drives dynamics among people involved in long distance relationships.

Exploring design opportunities

This project tells a story about users of Satily - imaginative service for improving the remote connectivity of family members living in distant geographical locations. For this project, I have speculated how to use artefacts to translate weather and support communicating love and care through their interfaces. In the explored scenario, Satily helps family members back home know what are the environmental conditions surrounding their loved ones who live away and enables them to send a friendly signal to communicate their concern.
