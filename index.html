Challenge

To enhance the user experience by designing clear and user-friendly interface and user interaction features. Embracing transparency and functionality of the app, my proposition promotes financial responsibility and independence.

Account page view

Visual hierarchy

  • Organised view that uses an interactive timeline helps to simplify and present essential information in a way that is receptive of distinct needs and end-goals of users.
  • Visual representation of data using graphs and bars helps users in a quick and effective identification of ratio between their income and outcome.

Function: The Overview function helps users in quick access to a summary of users income and outcome. Users can examine their spending across time.

User Oriented

  • Dynamic and responsive notifications allow users to efficiently move across the app to address a problematic area.
  • Directing users attention by implementing features that expand and highlight certain areas to help in guiding users through completing an action.

Function: Money Boxes function allows users to set personal or shared financial goals and collect money overtime by contributing directly to a money box or by scheduling a payment.

Payments page view
Drop down selection

Responsive

  • Collapsing and expanding menu allows for a quick access to a selection of features from any point in the app.
  • Responsive and accessible money and account management helps in allowing users to control their accounts on demand.

Function: Drop Down Menu allows users to quickly overview their accounts, access and transfer funds between current and savings accounts and manage their account on the go.

Transparent

  • A clear and accessible overview of money flow through a list of other contributors and their contributions.
  • Simplified, visual elements such as colour coded bars or collection deadlines help users in a more effective understanding of their financial goals.

Function: Money Boxes function allows users to set personal or shared financial goals and collect money overtime by contributing directly to a money box or by scheduling a payment.

MoneyBox detailed view

Engaging

  • Promoting meaning-making attitude by presenting users with visualisations of their goals by using photos and graphs.
  • Encouraging user participation and fostering the sense of community by enabling users to interact and follow actions of other contributors to grow excitement around their shared financial goal.

Function: Shared Money Boxes include a comment section with the most recent comments from collection participants.

Organised

  • Targeting and highlighting specific positions on the graph by assigning coloured tags and dividing spendings by categories allow users an access to a clear and organised view.

Design toolkit for NHS hospitals

  • user research
  • layout design
  • prototyping and user testing
Organisations
The Glasgow School of Art, The University Hospital Crosshouse
Timeline
5 weeks in 2016
Software
Sketch, Photoshop, Principle

Challenge

To improve quality of work for hospital staff and experience of care for hospital patients through research and analysis of current patient flow. Finding pain points and corresponding design opportunities in the system, creating and testing solutions while working closely with a spectrum of stakeholders.

Patient flow that delivers the right care by the right professional in the right place at the right time is a major challenge within the growing complexity of today’s health service and directly impacts on patient safety, quality of care and outcomes.

This open source, collaborative project resulted in development of a set of tools that requires the application of staff knowledge in order to identify challenges within the area of patient flow with a potential to expand use of designed toolkit and taught design thinking methods towards solving other problems within the hospital system.

For the final outcome, my group created a set of insight cards and insight cards templates for a user collaboration engagement kit for identifying challenges within the area of hospital patient flow in order to improve the hospital experience for patients and staff alike.

My main responsbility for this part of the project was to co-create layout for insight cards. I have also used my drawing skills to draw engaging and approachable illustrations to clearly communicate collected research used for insight cards.

Platform for social empowerment

  • user and contextual research
  • ideation
  • service design
Organisations
The Glasgow School of Art
Timeline
12 weeks in 2017
Software
Sketch, Photoshop, Illustrator

Challenge

I like to see non-conforming individuals being empowered and their social mobility improved. Gender non-conformists are a group who is struggling with this issue in particular, therefore I started this project where I explore and respond to complex values, needs and desires within social, political and community contexts.

Be-U comprises three main elements: Be-U app, Be-U toolkit and Be-U pride badge.

These touchpoints connect community members allowing them to embrace pride in unique and personal journey patterns of self-discovery. Be-U empowers the community by providing a responsive platform complemented by engagement tools for discussing, reflecting and sharing a spectrum of personal and collective experiences of gender non-conforming community and their allies (support network).

Organisations
The Glasgow School of Art
Timeline
4 weeks in 2017
Software
Photoshop, Illustrator

Challenge

To create an 'Alternative Now' scenario in which Low Earth Orbit Satellite Technology drives social and personal dynamics on Earth.

This project tells a story about users of Satily - imaginative service aimed at improving remote connectivity of family members living in distant geographical locations. In explored scenarios, Satily helps family members back home know what are the environmental conditions surrounding their loved ones who are away.
